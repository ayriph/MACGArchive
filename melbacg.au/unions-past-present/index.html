Unions: Past & Present

We’re starting up a new discussion group called Unions: Past & Present!

We’ll be looking at range of historical union struggles in Australia from the Clarrie O’Shea general strike in 1969, to the BLF’s green bans and the 1998 Waterfront dispute. We’ll talk about the lessons we can learn from these disputes and how they apply to contemporary examples of organising like the 2021 Geelong Library strikes. We’ll also discuss broader union theory and strategy and the organising context we find ourselves in today.

We will assume a little bit of background knowledge, so it’s open to anyone who has already completed our introductory education sequence – the Anarchist Political School.

All sessions will consist of a reading, which we read and discuss together, as well as some optional additional reading to provide more context if you’re particularly interested. Unions: Past & Present is completely free. All sessions are run in a wheelchair accessible location nearby to public transport. Each discussion goes for 90 minutes.

To participate and for more information, fill in the expression of interest form below, or send us an email at melbourne@ancomfed.org!
